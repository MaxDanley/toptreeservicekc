Key Takeaways

  • Winter often supports structural pruning for visibility and cleaner branch architecture.
  • Spring trims should focus on safety and selective growth management.
  • Summer and fall schedules should prioritize clearance and weak limb removal.

Winter trimming plan

Dormant-season work improves branch visibility and helps shape future growth.

  • Remove crossing limbs
  • Address overextended weight loads
  • Set roof and driveway clearance

In-season maintenance

During active growth, limit overcutting and focus on high-risk corrections.

  • Target dead or damaged limbs
  • Reduce storm-prone overhangs
  • Preserve healthy crown balance

Action Checklist

  • Match trim type to season
  • Avoid over-pruning
  • Recheck storm-exposed limbs

Guide FAQ

When is trimming safest?

Dormant season is often preferred for structure, but hazard limbs should be handled any time.

Can trimming stop storm damage?

It cannot eliminate risk, but strategic pruning significantly reduces failure points.

How often should mature trees be inspected?

At least annually, and after major wind or ice events.
