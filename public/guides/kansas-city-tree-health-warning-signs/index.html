Key Takeaways

  • Leaf thinning and dieback patterns can signal deeper structural issues.
  • Fungal growth at the base often warrants professional inspection.
  • Trunk cavities and bark separation should be evaluated quickly.

Visual stress indicators

Some symptoms are seasonal; others indicate active decline that needs immediate follow-up.

  • Progressive canopy thinning
  • Dead branch clusters
  • Uneven leaf-out

When to escalate

Signs near structures or walkways should be prioritized for quick assessment.

  • Root heave signs
  • Major branch cracks
  • Repeated limb drop

Action Checklist

  • Track visible changes
  • Photograph suspect areas
  • Book inspection after storms

Guide FAQ

Are all dead branches dangerous?

Not all, but larger deadwood over traffic areas should be addressed promptly.

Can health care avoid removal?

Sometimes. Early intervention improves the chance of preserving trees.

How often should I inspect?

At least once yearly plus after major weather events.
