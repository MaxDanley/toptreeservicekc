Key Takeaways

  • Grinding is usually faster and causes less yard disturbance.
  • Full extraction can help if you need immediate replanting in the same footprint.
  • Root-zone behavior and fill strategy should be clarified before work starts.

Best use cases for grinding

Grinding works well for most homeowners who want a clean lawn restoration path.

  • Lower disruption to surrounding soil
  • Fast completion on standard stumps
  • Lower equipment footprint

When full removal helps

Extraction may be useful for hardscape projects or immediate root-zone rebuilds.

  • Large construction plans
  • Immediate regrade requirements
  • Root conflict with future install

Action Checklist

  • Confirm grind depth
  • Ask about chips and fill
  • Document final finish standard

Guide FAQ

Is grinding enough for sod?

Usually yes, if depth and backfill are handled properly.

Will roots keep growing?

Some roots may decay over time. Ask about expected settlement and fill planning.

Does extraction cost more?

It often does due to equipment and restoration scope.
